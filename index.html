Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Income

By Terra Vaughn

New data shows a moderate, negative correlation between obesity and median income for adults in the United States. The scatter plot shows that as the median household income increases, the obesity rate decreases. This could be due to a lack of healthy foods (i.e. food deserts) in impoverished communities. Further studies need to be completed to identify if lack of appropriate health care and access to recreational activities also plays a part in this phenomenon.
